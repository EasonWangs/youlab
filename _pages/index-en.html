Welcome to the You Group at Westlake University!

Scientific Vision

Many important processes in biology and materials science emerge not from static structures alone, but from molecular motion, transient interactions, and dynamic fluctuations. You lab develops and applies advanced spectroscopic approaches to study hydrogen-bond dynamics, interfacial hydration, molecular organization, and nonequilibrium processes in heterogeneous environments. We are particularly interested in systems where local interactions evolve collectively across multiple spatial and temporal scales.

Our research focuses on molecular dynamics, interfacial interactions, and spatiotemporal heterogeneity in complex biological and material systems. By integrating ultrafast vibrational spectroscopy, nanoscale spectroscopic imaging, and multiscale simulations, we aim to understand how local molecular interactions evolve across space and time, and how these dynamic processes give rise to structure, function, and emergent behavior.

Principal Investigator (PI)


Meet Xiao You

Xiao You is a Westlake Fellow and Principal Investigator at the School of Engineering, Westlake University. She received her B.S. in Macromolecular Science from Fudan University, her Ph.D. in Materials Science from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill (advised by Joanna Atkin), and completed postdoctoral training at the University of Texas at Austin (Carlos Baiz) and Stanford University / SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory. She joined Westlake University in 2023.
Her research focuses on molecular dynamics, interfacial interactions, and spatiotemporal heterogeneity in complex biological and material systems. The You Lab combines ultrafast vibrational spectroscopy, nanoscale spectroscopic imaging, and multiscale simulations to investigate how local molecular interactions evolve across space and time. Dr. You has secured ¥3.3M in competitive funding as Lead PI, including support from the NSFC Young Scientists Fund and the Zhejiang Provincial Talent Program.
